What is Categorical Data?

Categorical data (also called qualitative data) represents characteristics. It puts data into groups or categories rather than measuring a quantity.

It Describes

Answers questions like "What type?", "Which one?", or "How would you rate it?"

No "True" Math

You can't calculate an "average" hair color or zip code. Mathematical operations don't apply directly.

The Classification

Nominal vs. Ordinal

The two main subtypes. The difference lies in order.

Tricky Case: Zip Codes & Phone Numbers

Even though they are made of numbers, Zip Codes are actually Categorical (Nominal). Why? Because the "average" of two zip codes (e.g., 90210 + 10001 / 2) is meaningless. They are just labels for a place.
